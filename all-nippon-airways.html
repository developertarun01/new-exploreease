      About All Nippon Airways

      All Nippon Airways (ANA), established in 1952, is one of the largest airlines in Japan and a leading full-service carrier in Asia, with headquarters located in Tokyo. The airline connects travelers to more than 90 destinations across domestic Japan and international routes throughout Asia, Europe, North America, and Oceania. ANA operates major hubs at Tokyo Haneda, Tokyo Narita, and Osaka Kansai International Airport. Exploreease allows passengers to complete All Nippon Airways flight reservations conveniently online. Travelers can easily search and book competitive ANA flight tickets through our platform. We also highlight selected All Nippon Airways flight deals on popular routes, helping travelers manage travel expenses efficiently. The airline is a member of the Star Alliance network and maintains codeshare partnerships with numerous global carriers, enabling seamless connections to major destinations worldwide.

      ANA Mileage Club is the airline’s loyalty rewards program designed to help members earn and redeem mileage points across flights and partner services.       All Nippon Airways Baggage Policy

      Checked baggage allowance depends on travel class, route, and ANA Mileage Club membership level. Baggage rules may vary depending on domestic or international itineraries.
      1. Carry-On Baggage: Passengers are typically permitted one carry-on bag along with one personal item free of charge. The carry-on dimensions must generally not exceed 55 cm x 40 cm x 25 cm including wheels and handles.
      Personal items should fit under the seat in front of the passenger.
      2. Checked Baggage: Complimentary checked baggage is usually included on most international fares. Economy Class passengers may carry bags up to 23 kg each, while Business and First Class may allow up to 32 kg per bag depending on route.
      ANA Mileage Club elite members may receive additional baggage privileges.
      Special baggage such as sports equipment or musical instruments may be transported according to airline regulations.
