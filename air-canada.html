      About Air Canada

      Air Canada, founded in 1937, is the flag carrier and largest airline of Canada, with its corporate headquarters based in Montreal, Quebec. The airline connects passengers to more than 180 destinations worldwide across domestic, transborder, and international routes. Its primary operational hubs include Toronto Pearson, Montreal–Trudeau, Vancouver, and Calgary, with Toronto serving as the largest gateway for global travel. Exploreease enables travelers to conveniently complete Air Canada flight reservations online. Users can quickly search and secure competitive Air Canada flight tickets through our platform. We also highlight selected Air Canada flight offers on popular routes, helping passengers manage travel costs efficiently. Air Canada is a member of the Star Alliance network and maintains codeshare agreements with multiple international airlines, allowing seamless connectivity to major cities across the world.

      Services & Amenities

      Air Canada Check-in Methods

      Passengers may complete check-in for Air Canada flights either at airport service counters or through digital check-in options. Online check-in is generally preferred as it saves time and simplifies the boarding process, although certain travel situations may require in-person verification at the airport. For check-in assistance or boarding pass support, travelers may contact Exploreease customer service.
      1. Airport Check-in: Travelers must arrive early to complete formalities at the airport. For domestic flights, check-in is typically required at least 45 minutes before departure, while international passengers should finalize the process 60–90 minutes in advance depending on airport guidelines. These timings are general recommendations, and passengers should confirm exact deadlines prior to travel.
      2. Online Check-in: Air Canada online check-in opens 24 hours before scheduled departure and is available through the airline’s official website or mobile application, allowing passengers to select seats and receive digital boarding passes.

      Air Canada Frequent Flyer Program

      Aeroplan is Air Canada’s loyalty rewards program designed to help members earn and redeem points through flights and partner services. Members can collect points by traveling with Air Canada and partner airlines, hotel bookings, car rentals, everyday purchases, and approved financial partners associated with the program.
      Accumulated points can later be redeemed for flight rewards, travel upgrades, or additional benefits.
      The program includes multiple status levels such as 25K, 35K, 50K, 75K, and Super Elite, offering increasing travel privileges.

      Baggage Allowance & Booking

      Air Canada Baggage Policy

      Checked baggage eligibility depends on fare category, travel route, cabin class, and Aeroplan status level. Applicable fees may vary depending on ticket type and destination.
      1. Carry-On Baggage: Passengers are generally allowed one standard carry-on bag and one personal item free of charge. Carry-on dimensions must not exceed 55 cm x 23 cm x 40 cm.
      Personal items should remain within 43 cm x 33 cm x 16 cm to fit under the seat.
      2. Checked Baggage: Complimentary checked baggage may or may not be included depending on fare selection. Standard checked bags must not exceed 158 cm in total linear dimensions. Economy class usually permits bags weighing up to 23 kg each, while premium cabins may allow up to 32 kg per bag.
      Aeroplan elite members may receive additional baggage benefits based on status.
      Special items such as sports equipment or musical instruments can be transported according to airline guidelines.
      Passengers may add checked baggage during the Air Canada booking process through our platform.

      Air Canada provides a streamlined and modern booking experience through its official website, allowing travelers to search, compare, and reserve flights across an extensive global network. The booking system enables users to choose travel dates, cabin options, and fare bundles while viewing transparent pricing and schedules. Passengers can manage reservations online, select preferred seating, purchase travel extras, and complete payments using standard methods or Aeroplan points.       Airline Changes & Cancellation

      Air Canada Flight Changes

      Air Canada permits itinerary adjustments depending on fare family, route, cabin class, and ticket rules selected during booking. Many Standard, Flex, Comfort, and premium cabin fares allow passengers to modify travel dates, departure times, or destinations using the manage booking feature, while Basic fares may have more limited flexibility. Same-day flight changes may be available on eligible routes subject to availability and applicable conditions. Any voluntary modification may require payment of fare differences, taxes, or surcharges if the replacement flight costs more. International bookings can follow specific change rules based on fare category. Reviewing fare conditions before requesting updates helps travelers manage plans efficiently and avoid unexpected charges or travel disruptions.

      Air Canada Flight Cancellation

      Air Canada offers cancellation options designed to provide flexibility for changing travel plans. Tickets canceled within 24 hours of purchase are typically eligible for a full refund when booked at least seven days before departure. Refundable fares qualify for monetary refunds, while most non-refundable tickets may provide travel credits for future use instead of cash reimbursement. Cancellation and change fees depend on fare type, with more flexible fare bundles offering greater freedom. If Air Canada cancels a flight or significantly alters the schedule, passengers may request refunds or alternative travel arrangements. Travel credits issued after voluntary cancellations generally remain valid for a limited period, allowing passengers to rebook later. Policies can vary for international journeys or promotional fares, so reviewing ticket conditions before canceling is recommended.

      International Flights & Routes

      Air Canada International Flights

      Air Canada operates an extensive international network connecting travelers from major Canadian hubs such as Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal, and Calgary to destinations worldwide. The airline offers long-haul services across Europe, Asia, the Middle East, South America, and Oceania, with multiple cabin options including Economy, Premium Economy, Business Class, and Signature Class for enhanced comfort on intercontinental journeys. International flights often include onboard meals, entertainment systems, and expanded baggage allowances compared to shorter domestic routes. As a member of the Star Alliance, Air Canada provides expanded global connectivity through partner airlines, enabling smooth transfers and coordinated travel experiences across continents. This partnership network allows passengers to access hundreds of destinations worldwide through shared schedules and loyalty benefits.

      Air Canada International Routes

      Air Canada maintains a broad international route network linking Canada with key global cities. Popular nonstop routes include flights from Canadian hubs to destinations such as London, Paris, Frankfurt, Tokyo, Delhi, Dubai (via partners), and major cities throughout the United States, Mexico, and South America. Routes are adjusted regularly based on seasonal demand, passenger preferences, and operational planning while expanding connectivity through alliance partnerships. Many international services are structured to support both business and leisure travel with convenient connection timings. Through Star Alliance cooperation, passengers can continue journeys to regional destinations without separate bookings. This integrated routing structure strengthens Air Canada’s worldwide presence and allows travelers to plan complex international itineraries with coordinated baggage transfers, shared lounges, and synchronized flight connections.
