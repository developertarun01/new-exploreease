      About Air Canada

      Air Canada, founded in 1937, is the flag carrier of Canada, with its corporate headquarters in Montreal, Quebec. The airline operates an extensive network serving more than 180 destinations across domestic, transborder, and international markets. Major hubs include Toronto Pearson, Montreal-Trudeau, Vancouver, and Calgary airports. Exploreease enables travelers to complete Air Canada flight reservations easily through an online booking experience. Passengers can quickly search and secure competitive Air Canada ticket options using our platform. We also present selected promotional Air Canada flight deals on specific routes, helping travelers manage overall travel costs efficiently. The airline is a founding member of the Star Alliance and maintains partnerships with numerous global carriers, allowing passengers to connect seamlessly to hundreds of destinations worldwide.

      Services and Amenities

      Air Canada Check-in Methods

      Passengers may check in for Air Canada flights either at airport counters or through digital check-in services. Online check-in is generally recommended because it reduces wait times and simplifies the boarding process. However, certain travel situations may still require airport check-in assistance. For any help related to boarding passes or check-in procedures, travelers can contact Exploreease customer support.
      1. Airport Check-in: Travelers must arrive at the airport to complete required formalities. For domestic flights, check-in usually closes 45 minutes before departure, while international flights typically require completion at least 60 minutes prior. Passengers should always verify timing requirements before travel.
      2. Online Check-in: Air Canada online check-in opens 24 hours before departure and generally remains available up to 60 minutes before scheduled takeoff. Passengers may check in through the airline’s official website or mobile app.

      Air Canada Frequent Flyer Program

      Aeroplan is Air Canada’s loyalty program designed to reward frequent travelers through points earning and redemption opportunities. Members can collect points by flying with Air Canada and partner airlines, as well as through hotel stays, vacation packages, car rentals, co-branded credit cards, and participating retail partners.
      Accumulated points can later be redeemed for flight rewards, upgrades, or travel-related benefits.
      The program features multiple status levels, including 25K, 35K, 50K, 75K, and Super Elite tiers.

      Baggage Allowance

      Air Canada Baggage Policy

      Air Canada baggage allowances vary depending on fare category, travel route, cabin class, and Aeroplan status level.
      1. Carry-On Baggage: Passengers are permitted one standard carry-on bag along with one personal item without additional charge. Maximum carry-on dimensions are 9 inches (23 cm) x 15.5 inches (40 cm) x 21.5 inches (55 cm).
      Personal items must generally not exceed 6 inches (16 cm) x 13 inches (33 cm) x 17 inches (43 cm).
      2. Checked Baggage: Complimentary checked baggage depends on fare type and destination. Travelers may usually check bags with maximum linear dimensions of 62 inches and a weight limit of 50 lbs each. Premium cabins and elite members may receive higher allowances up to 70 lbs per bag.
      Aeroplan Elite Status members may qualify for additional baggage benefits depending on tier.
      Special items such as sporting equipment or musical instruments may be accepted according to airline guidelines.
      Passengers can add checked baggage while booking Air Canada flights through our platform.

      Airline Charges & Cancellation

      Airline Charges

      Air Canada applies varying service charges based on fare family, route, cabin selection, and optional services chosen by travelers. Base fares generally include a personal item and carry-on baggage, while services such as checked baggage, preferred seating, onboard Wi-Fi, and upgrades may require additional payment. Economy fares on select routes may include baggage fees aligned with standard industry pricing, whereas premium cabins and elite members often receive complimentary baggage allowances. Many international itineraries include at least one checked bag depending on destination and fare bundle. Fees may also apply for same-day flight changes, seat selection, or extra-legroom seating options. Fare differences must be paid when modifying reservations even if change fees are waived. Reviewing fare conditions in advance helps travelers avoid unexpected costs and plan travel expenses more effectively.

      Airline Cancellation

      Air Canada provides flexible cancellation options designed to offer passengers greater control over their travel plans. Tickets canceled within 24 hours of purchase are eligible for a full refund provided the booking was made at least seven days before departure. Refundable fares typically qualify for cash refunds, while most non-refundable tickets are issued as future travel credits. Change and cancellation fees are waived for many standard and premium fare categories, although Basic fares may carry certain restrictions. If Air Canada cancels a flight or makes a significant schedule adjustment, passengers may request refunds or alternative travel arrangements. Travel credits issued after voluntary cancellations usually remain valid for a limited time period, allowing rebooking at a later date. Policies may differ slightly for international routes or promotional fares, so reviewing ticket terms before canceling remains important.

      International Flights & Routes

      International Flights

      Air Canada operates one of the largest international networks among Canadian airlines, connecting travelers through hubs such as Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver, and Calgary to destinations worldwide. The airline provides long-haul services across Europe, Asia, the Middle East, Oceania, and the Americas, offering cabin options including Economy, Premium Economy, and Air Canada Signature Class for enhanced comfort on intercontinental routes. International flights often include additional onboard services such as meals, entertainment systems, and baggage benefits compared with domestic travel. As a founding member of the Star Alliance, Air Canada enables extended global connectivity through partner airlines, ensuring smooth transfers and coordinated travel experiences across continents.

      International Routes

      Air Canada maintains a wide international route network linking North America with major global cities. Popular routes include nonstop flights to destinations such as London, Paris, Frankfurt, Tokyo, Delhi, Sydney, and numerous locations across the United States, Europe, Asia, and South America. The airline regularly adjusts routes based on seasonal demand, passenger trends, and operational planning while expanding connectivity through alliance partnerships. Many international schedules are designed for both business and leisure travelers, providing convenient connections at major hubs. Through Star Alliance cooperation, passengers can continue journeys to regional destinations without separate bookings. This integrated network strengthens Air Canada’s global presence and allows travelers to plan complex international itineraries with coordinated baggage handling, shared lounges, and synchronized connections.
