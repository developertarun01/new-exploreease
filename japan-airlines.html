      About Japan Airlines

      Japan Airlines, commonly known as JAL, was founded in 1951 and is one of the leading full-service carriers of Japan, with headquarters located in Tokyo. The airline operates an extensive domestic and international network connecting major destinations across Asia, Europe, North America, and Oceania. Its primary hubs are Tokyo Haneda Airport and Tokyo Narita International Airport, supported by key operations in Osaka and other Japanese cities. Exploreease allows travelers to make Japan Airlines flight reservations easily through a convenient online platform. We also feature selected Japan Airlines flight deals on popular routes, helping travelers plan cost-effective journeys. The airline is a member of the oneworld alliance and maintains codeshare partnerships with several global carriers, enabling smooth worldwide connectivity.

      Services and Amenities

      Japan Airlines Check-in Methods

      Passengers may complete check-in for Japan Airlines flights either at airport counters or through digital check-in services. Online and mobile check-in options are generally preferred as they provide flexibility and help passengers save time before departure. In certain travel situations, airport counter check-in may still be required. For any check-in or boarding pass assistance, travelers may contact Exploreease customer support.
      1. Airport Check-in: Travelers must present required travel documents at the airport counter to complete check-in procedures. For domestic flights, counters usually close around 30 minutes before departure, while international flights typically close about 60 minutes prior. Passengers should verify exact deadlines before travel.
      2. Online Check-in: Japan Airlines online check-in generally opens 24 hours before departure and remains available until approximately 1 hour before scheduled takeoff. Passengers can check in using the airline’s official website or mobile application.

      Japan Airlines Frequent Flyer Program

      JAL Mileage Bank is the airline’s loyalty rewards program designed to provide members with mileage earning and redemption opportunities. Members can earn miles through flights with Japan Airlines and oneworld partner airlines, as well as through hotels, car rentals, credit cards, and lifestyle partners associated with the program.
      Accumulated miles may be redeemed for award travel, cabin upgrades, and additional travel benefits.
      The program includes multiple membership tiers such as Crystal, Sapphire, and Diamond, offering enhanced privileges including priority services and additional baggage allowance.

      Baggage Allowance

      Japan Airlines Baggage Policy

      Japan Airlines generally includes complimentary checked baggage allowance, depending on route, fare type, and travel class. Allowances may follow either the piece or weight concept based on destination regulations.
      1. Carry-On Baggage: Passengers are typically permitted one cabin bag along with one personal item at no additional cost. Cabin baggage must meet airline size requirements and usually weigh up to 10 kg in Economy Class, while premium cabins may allow additional allowance.
      Personal items such as handbags or laptop bags must fit under the seat or within overhead compartments.
      2. Checked Baggage: Complimentary checked baggage is included on most international fares. Economy Class passengers are generally allowed at least two checked bags on long-haul routes, while Business and First Class passengers receive higher baggage limits. Weight and size restrictions apply according to airline guidelines.
      Frequent flyer elite members may receive additional baggage privileges depending on membership level.
      Special baggage such as sports equipment or musical instruments may be transported according to airline regulations.
      Passengers can add or manage baggage while booking Japan Airlines flight tickets through our platform.

      Airline Charges & Cancellation

      Airline Charges

      Japan Airlines (JAL) applies airline charges based on fare family, travel class, destination, and optional services selected during booking. Most international tickets include a carry-on bag and checked baggage allowance depending on cabin class, with higher limits provided for Premium Economy, Business Class, and First Class passengers. Additional fees may apply for excess baggage, seat upgrades, special meals, and optional airport services. Government taxes, airport facility charges, and international surcharges are included depending on departure country regulations. JAL follows a fare-rule system, meaning charges for changes or upgrades vary according to ticket flexibility. Optional services purchased separately may not always be refundable once used. Understanding fare conditions before travel helps passengers manage expenses and avoid unexpected airline charges during international journeys.

      Airline Cancellation

      Japan Airlines provides cancellation and refund options according to ticket rules and timing of the request. Eligible tickets booked at least seven days before departure may be canceled within 24 hours of purchase for a full refund without penalty in applicable regions. After this period, refunds are calculated by deducting cancellation charges specified in the fare rules, and passengers must usually cancel before departure to remain eligible for refunds. Changes and cancellations can be completed online, through call centers, or airport counters. If JAL cancels a flight or significantly changes schedules, passengers are offered rebooking or refunds for unused segments. In cases where delays or cancellations occur due to airline responsibility, accommodation or transportation expenses may also be reimbursed within set limits.

      International Flights & Routes

      International Flights

      Japan Airlines operates an extensive international flight network connecting Japan with major destinations across Asia, North America, Europe, and Oceania. Long-haul international services primarily operate from Tokyo Haneda and Tokyo Narita airports, supported by Osaka and other regional gateways. Passengers can choose from Economy Class, Premium Economy, Business Class, and First Class cabins, with onboard amenities including meals, entertainment systems, and generous baggage allowances on most international routes. JAL is a member of the oneworld alliance, allowing travelers to connect seamlessly with partner airlines worldwide and access shared loyalty benefits, coordinated schedules, and global lounge networks. This partnership significantly expands travel options beyond JAL-operated flights while maintaining consistent service standards across international journeys.

      International Routes

      Japan Airlines maintains a strong international route structure linking Japan with major global cities such as New York, Los Angeles, London, Paris, Sydney, Bangkok, Singapore, and Delhi through direct or partner-operated connections. The airline’s hub-and-spoke network enables efficient transfers between domestic Japanese cities and long-haul intercontinental flights. Routes are adjusted seasonally to match tourism demand and business travel trends while maintaining connectivity between Asia and North America in particular. Through codeshare agreements and oneworld alliance partnerships, passengers can continue journeys to hundreds of additional destinations under a single booking with coordinated baggage transfers and travel benefits. This integrated global network supports both leisure and corporate travelers seeking reliable international connectivity.
