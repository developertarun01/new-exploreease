      About Thai Airways

      Thai Airways International, established in 1960, is the national carrier of Thailand and one of Southeast Asia’s prominent full-service airlines. Headquartered in Bangkok, the airline operates its primary hub at Suvarnabhumi Airport, connecting travelers to destinations across Asia, Europe, Australia, and the Middle East. Thai Airways is widely recognized for its Thai-inspired hospitality, full-service onboard experience, and strong international presence. Exploreease allows travelers to make Thai Airways flight reservations conveniently through an easy online booking process. The airline is a member of the Star Alliance and maintains codeshare partnerships with several global carriers, enabling seamless connectivity to destinations worldwide.

      Thai Airways Check-in Methods

      Passengers traveling with Thai Airways may complete check-in through airport counters, self-service kiosks, or online check-in options. Online check-in is generally preferred as it reduces waiting time and allows travelers to confirm seats before arriving at the airport. Certain travel situations, including document verification or special assistance, may require airport check-in.       1. Airport Check-in: Travelers are advised to arrive early to complete procedures smoothly. Check-in for most international flights typically closes about 60 minutes before departure, although timings may vary depending on airport regulations and destination requirements.

      Thai Airways Frequent Flyer Program

      Royal Orchid Plus is Thai Airways’ loyalty program designed to reward frequent travelers through mileage earning and redemption opportunities. Members earn miles when flying with Thai Airways or Star Alliance partner airlines, along with hotel stays, car rentals, and lifestyle partners.
      Miles may be redeemed for award flights, cabin upgrades, and additional travel benefits.
      The program includes membership tiers such as Silver, Gold, and Platinum, offering enhanced privileges including priority services and lounge access.

      Thai Airways Baggage Policy

      Baggage allowances vary depending on fare type, travel class, and route. Policies may differ between regional and long-haul international flights.
      1. Carry-On Baggage: Passengers are generally permitted one cabin bag along with a personal item at no additional cost. Cabin baggage dimensions typically should not exceed 56 cm x 45 cm x 25 cm, with weight limits varying according to cabin class.
      2. Checked Baggage: Complimentary checked baggage is included on most fares. Economy Class passengers commonly receive allowances ranging from 20 kg to 30 kg depending on fare category, while Business Class passengers receive higher baggage limits.
      Special baggage such as sports equipment or musical instruments may be transported according to airline regulations.

      Thai Airways operates an extensive international flight network connecting Bangkok with major cities across Asia, Europe, Australia, and the Middle East.
